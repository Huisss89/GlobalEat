Kimchi 김치

Korean | By Kenji Nakamura

Kimchi is a traditional Korean side dish consisting of salted and fermented vegetables, most often napa cabbage or Korean radish. A wide selection of seasonings is used, including gochugaru, spring onions, garlic, ginger, and jeotgal. Kimchi is also used in a variety of soups and stews. Kimchi is a staple food in Korean cuisine and is eaten as a side dish with almost every Korean meal.

Recipe Ingredients

  • kosher salt for curing
  • 1 large napa cabbage
  • 2 medium sized carrots
  • 1 small daikon radish
  • 1 bunch green onion
  • 1 large asian pear
  • 6 cloves garlic - peeled
  • 2 inch piece ginger - peeled
  • 60ml fish sauce
  • 65g Korean red pepper flakes

Recipe Instruction

  1. Cut the napa cabbage in half. Repeat for 3 times.
  2. Cut the cabbage quarters into 1 inch wide strips.
  3. Put the cabbage in a large bowl, heavily seasoning with kosher salt.
  4. Toss them really hard, brush it. Leave it for 30 minutes.
  5. Cut the toufu into small cubes (1/2 inch)
  6. Toss the tofu in a pot of salted water (2 tsp of salt) for 3 minutes.
  7. Cut the green onion into 1/2 inch parts and carrots in julienne style.
  8. Peel the daikon and chop in a thicker julienne style.
  9. Slice 2 inch segment of peeled ginger and place into a food processor.
  10. Peel and cut the asian pear into cubes and put in the processor.
  11. Add 6 cloves of peeled garlic and 60ml of fish sauce in the processor.
  12. Blend until smooth, transfer into another bowl
  13. Mix with korean red pepper flakes.
  14. After 30 minutes of sitting, drain the cabbage through colander
  15. Rinse with water while squeezing at the same time.
  16. Toss and squeeze until majority of the salt has been washed away.
  17. Add the cabbage, all the vegetables, and the paste into a large bowl.
  18. Toss till thoroughly coat and pack into a gallon jar.
  19. Use a spoon to press down the vegetables to remove the air bubbles.
  20. Loosely cap the jar off and sit for 4-7 days.
  21. Make sure to press it throughout to remove air bubbles.
  22. Tighten the cap and store in fridge once it flavour hit the right note.

Comment From ME

As someone who’s spent time mastering the art of kimchi in Seoul, I can’t emphasize enough how this dish is a staple in Korean cuisine and an essential part of any meal.
The balance of spicy, tangy, and umami flavors in a well-made kimchi can elevate even the simplest of dishes. Whether you’re enjoying it as a side or incorporating it into a recipe like kimchi fried rice, the depth of flavor is unparalleled.
For the best experience, I recommend making it at home if you can—there's something truly special about enjoying a batch of homemade kimchi that you've fermented to perfection.
