ZhaJiangMian 炸酱面

China | By Li Wei Chen

Zha Jiang Mian 炸酱面 commonly translated as "noodles served with fried bean sauce",is a dish of Chinese origin consisting of thick wheat noodles topped with zhajiang, a fermented soybean-based sauce. Variations may include toppings of fresh or pickled vegetables, beans, meat, tofu, or egg. In the Western press, it is occasionally dubbed "Beijing bolognaise" due to its superficial similarity (both dishes involve noodles with minced meat sauce) and ubiquitous nature.

Recipe Ingredients

  • 340g ground pork - chopped
  • 1 tablespoon minced ginger
  • 2 tablespoons Shaoxing wine
  • 200g sweet bean paste (Tian Mian Jiang)
  • 340ml water
  • 4 tablespoons vegetable oil (or peanut oil)
  • 1 white onion - diced
  • 340g fresh wheat noodles (or 240g dried wheat noodles)
  • 1 cucumber - julienned
  • Chopped green onion - for garnish
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Stir fried tomato and egg (Optional)

Recipe Instruction

  1. Add sweet bean sauce to a large bowl.
  2. Slowly blend in water and stir it, till water is fully incorporated.
  3. Heat a large skillet or a wok over medium-high heat until hot.
  4. Add the oil and the ginger. Stir a few times to release the fragrance.
  5. Add the pork. Cook and stir, until the pork turns into browned pieces.
  6. Add the sweet bean sauce and turn to medium heat.
  7. Stir for 10 mins, until the sauce thickens and turns darker.
  8. Add the onion. Cook and stir until it has softened but remains crispy.
  9. Turn off heat and transfer the sauce to a large bowl.
  10. Boil a large pot of water and cook the noodles.
  11. Once done, drain the noodles immediately and rinse with cold water.
  12. Add noodles to individual serving bowls.
  13. Top noodles with sauce, cucumber and garnish with green onions.

Comment From ME

Zha Jiang Mian holds a special place in my heart. Growing up in Sichuan, I loved the rich, savory sauce made from ground pork and soybean paste.
The combination of chewy noodles and fresh veggies is so satisfying. It’s a great example of how even simple dishes can be full of flavor and tradition.
For me, Zha Jiang Mian isn’t just a meal; it’s a reminder of the rich tapestry of Chinese food culture and how even the most straightforward dishes can tell a story of tradition and innovation.
