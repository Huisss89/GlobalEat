Pasta

Italy | By Sophia Rossi

Italian pasta is a general term for a variety of foods produced from wheat flour and water, occasionally with eggs. The name relates to the resulting dough (pasta also means "dough"), which is rolled out and cut into various shapes. Pasta is cooked in water and served with sauce. Overall, pasta is one of the world's most well-known, popular, and beloved dishes.

Recipe Ingredients

  • 2 colves garlic
  • 15g fresh grated parmesan cheese
  • 15g untoasted pinenuts
  • 45g or 1 bunch basil leaves
  • 3 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 250g pasta
  • 1 1/2 tablespoon sea salt (1/2 tablespoon for pesto, 1 tablespoon for pasta water)

Recipe Instruction

  1. Place a saucepan filled with 3 quarters full of water onto a high heat.
  2. Add the garlic, fresly grated Parmesan Cheese, and pinenuts to a blender.
  3. Blitz these up for 20 seconds.
  4. Add the fresh basil leaves, extra virgin olive oil, sea salt, and black pepper to the blender.
  5. Blitz this until all of the ingredients are well blended.
  6. Add sea salt and pasta into the water and move the pasta around.
  7. After 11 minutes, scoop out 1 cup or 250 ml of pasta water.
  8. Remove the saucepan from the heat and drain it for 2 minutes.
  9. Add the pasta to a large mixing bowl and pour over the basil pesto.
  10. Add in 1/4 of a cup of the reserved pasta water and the starch in the pasta water.
  11. Add this to a serving bowl by twisting it in.
  12. Add some fresh Parmesan cheese and tear over some fresh basil leaves.

Comment From ME

Pasta is one of my favorite dishes! Whether it's a simple spaghetti with marinara sauce or a complex lasagna layered with cheese and meat, pasta always hits the spot.
I love how versatile it is—you can mix it with almost any ingredient and create a delicious meal. From creamy Alfredo to zesty pesto, there's a pasta dish for every craving. Plus, it's quick and easy to cook, making it a perfect choice for busy weeknights or fancy dinners.
