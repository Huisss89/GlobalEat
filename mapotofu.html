Mapo Tofu
麻婆豆腐

China | By Li Wei Chen

Mapo Tofu 麻婆豆腐 is a popular Chinese dish from the Sichuan province, known for its bold and spicy flavors. The dish typically consists of tofu set in a spicy, fragrant sauce, along with minced meat (usually pork or beef), doubanjiang,garlic, and Sichuan peppercorns, which give it a unique, numbing spiciness. This very flavourful tofu dish is made fast with simply a handful of ingredients and goes great with a bowl of steamed rice.

Recipe Ingredients

  • 2 tablebspoon Chinese Chili Bean Paste(Doubanjiang)
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Shaoxing wine(Rice wine)
  • 1 tablespoon black vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • 1 tablespoon chili oil
  • 3 cloves garlic - chopped
  • 2 oz ginger - chopped
  • 1 red chili - chopped (option)
  • 3 green onions - chopped
  • 1 teaspoon toasted and grind Sichuan peppercorn
  • 1/2 lb minced pork or beef
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • 1 block (14 oz) silken tofu, cut into 3/4-inch cubes
  • 1 tablespoon conrnstarch

Recipe Instruction

  1. Combine all the sauce, wine, vinegar, and sugar in a mixing bowl.
  2. Heat a cast iron wok over medium heat.
  3. Add the oil, chopped garlic, ginger, chili and green onions.
  4. Stir fry all together about 1 minute, put in Sichuan peppercorn powder.
  5. Pour sauce mixture,stir fry sauce and aromatic vegetables for 3 minutes.
  6. Pour stock and deglaze bottom of wok. Bring it to boil.
  7. Add cubed tofu and carefully stir to keep the shape of tofu.
  8. Mix cornstarch and 2 tbsp water.
  9. Put starch mixture into boiling mapo tofu base while carefully stirring.
  10. Garnish with chopped green onions and extra Sichuan peppercorn powder.

Comment From ME

Mapo Tofu is a dish that perfectly encapsulates the bold, spicy flavors that Sichuan cuisine is known for. Growing up in Sichuan, I’ve always been drawn to its fiery yet comforting nature.
The combination of silky tofu, ground pork, and the iconic Sichuan peppercorns creates a unique balance of heat and numbness, known as ‘mala,’ that tingles your taste buds in the best way possible.
Whether you’re new to Sichuan cuisine or a seasoned enthusiast, Mapo Tofu is a must-try dish that brings the warmth and vibrancy of Sichuan right into your kitchen.
