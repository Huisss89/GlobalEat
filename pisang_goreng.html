Banana Fritter (Pisang Goreng)

Malaysia | By Aisha Tan

Banana fritter, known as pisang goreng in Malaysia, is a fritter made by deep frying battered banana or plantain in hot cooking oil. It is typically consumed as a snack in the morning and afternoon. They are often sold by street vendors in Malaysia, although it is also offered as a product at storefronts.

Recipe Ingredients

  • 1 ¼ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • ¼ tablespoon vanilla powder
  • ½ cup milk
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tablespoons butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon rum flavouring
  • 4 ripe bananas, sliced
  • 2 cups oil for frying

Recipe Instruction

  1. Combine flour, sugar, and vanilla powder in a large bowl; make a well in the center. Pour milk, egg, melted butter, and rum flavoring into the well; mix until smooth. Fold in banana slices until evenly coated.
  2. Heat oil in a wok or deep fryer to 190°C.
  3. Drop banana mixture by tablespoon into hot oil. Fry in hot oil until golden brown and crispy, 10 to 15 minutes. Remove bananas from oil and drain them on paper towels. Serve hot.

Comment From ME

Pisang Goreng holds a special place in my heart as a quintessential Malaysian snack.
The combination of a crispy, golden batter with the sweet, soft banana inside is simply irresistible. From the classic batter to more innovative variations, like adding a touch of cinnamon or coconut flakes, it’s a dish that invites experimentation.
Pisang Goreng is a wonderful example of how simple ingredients can create something extraordinary, and I’m always excited to see how others put their spin on this beloved treat.
