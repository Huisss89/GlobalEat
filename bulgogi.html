Bulgogi 불고기

Korean | By Kenji Nakamura

Bulgogi is probably one of the most popular Korean dishes, with thinly sliced meat that has a smoky-sweet flavor. You can enjoy it broiled, grilled, or stir-fried. The beef is usually accompanied by lettuce wraps and gochujang (spicy red pepper paste) for wrapping and spicing up the meat.

Recipe Ingredients

  • 300g rib eye - thinly sliced beef
  • 1/2 medium onion - thinly sliced
  • 1 stalk green onion - thinly sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic - minced
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 2 1/2 tablespoon dark soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon canola oil
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil

Recipe Instruction

  1. Slice the beef as thin as possible, about 1cm to 2.5cm thick.
  2. Mince the garlic, thinly slice onion and green onion.
  3. Heat oil in a skillet over medium high heat.
  4. Sear the beef and onions.
  5. Add brown sugar to the meat when the meat starts to get cooked.
  6. Make a small hole in the middle of the pan.
  7. Add soy sauce, oyster sauce, garlic and ground black pepper to the hole.
  8. Let it bubble to make a smoked flavor to bulgogi.
  9. When the sauce bubbles, add garlic and green onion to the pan.
  10. Mix the sauce with the meat. Cook for about 2 minutes until fully cooked.
  11. Turn off the heat. Sprinkle sesame oil. Serve.

Comment From ME

Bulgogi is a fantastic dish with its sweet and savory flavors, and it’s a great representation of Korean cuisine. To achieve the best results, marinate the beef for at least 2 hours to let the flavors fully penetrate the meat.
Use a combination of soy sauce, sugar, sesame oil, and garlic in the marinade to get that perfect balance.
Additionally, don’t be afraid to add a bit of pear or apple juice to the marinade for a subtle sweetness and tenderization. Grill or pan-cook the beef on high heat for a deliciously caramelized exterior and juicy interior. Serve it with steamed rice and some fresh vegetables for a well-rounded and satisfying meal.
